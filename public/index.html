      How to play

      Getting started

      Host a game: type your name and tap Host a game on this phone. This phone becomes the game table — keep the page open. Share the QR code or the 4-letter code shown next so others can join.

      Join a game: type your name, then scan the host's QR code, or enter their 4-letter code in the code box and tap Join. Everyone needs internet — but not the same Wi-Fi.

      Can't connect? Tap Connection check on the home screen to see which part of the link is blocked. Most networks work; some guest/hotel Wi-Fi needs everyone on cellular or a phone hotspot.

      The rules

      On your turn, draw glass shards from the kiln one at a time. Every shard is revealed to all.

      Crack! Draw a colour you already hold this turn and your glass shatters — you lose every shard you drew and your turn ends.

      Prism shield. A prism is a wild. If you draw a colour you already hold but have a prism in hand, the prism is spent to absorb the clash instead of cracking — you keep going, minus one prism. Prisms never clash with each other, so you can stockpile shields.

      Chase the spectrum. Bank four or more distinct colours in a single turn for a bonus — the more you hold, the bigger it gets. Holding all six at once is a Perfect Spectrum, scored the instant you draw the sixth (prisms help you survive the clashes on the way there).

      Know when to stop. Stop before you crack and you keep every shard you drew — then set them into your 5×5 window. Touching shards may never share a colour (a prism sits anywhere), so a line of glass is always a mix of colours.

      Build bright lines. Each shard scores the length of the unbroken line it extends — both directions where a row and a column cross. A lone shard scores 1, so build long, connected lines.

      Discarding costs you. If a shard has no legal cell — or you simply don't want it — you may discard it, but each discarded shard costs −1 point. Place when you can; throw glass away only when you must.

      • + line length each shard scores the unbroken line it extends — both directions where a row and column cross
      • +3 socket filled with its matching colour
      • +8 completed single-colour diagonal, either corner-to-corner (prism is wild)
      • +3 / +6 / +12 bank 4 / 5 / all 6 colours in a turn (6 = Perfect Spectrum)
      • +10 first to finish their window · −1 each shard you discard

      When someone completes their window, the round is played out so all have had equal turns — then the brightest window wins.
